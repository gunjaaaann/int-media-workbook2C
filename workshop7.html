MEMORY GARDEN @ ACMI

Memory Garden is an interactive and immersive display of Australian home videos from the ACMI collection

when a viewer places their hand between the towers, a video is projected on to their hand, using it as a screen

this was quite a novel experience when I saw it first. I find it interesting that they chose to use a hand as the projection surface rather than a wall. it suggests a deliberate effort to create a more intimate and interactive experience for the user.

KINFOLK

These are a set of fantastic beings that viewers can bring to life by jumping or dancing.

"Created from an unending algorithm, some of the artworks in Beings are generative and evolve in real-time, so that no two visits are the same."
