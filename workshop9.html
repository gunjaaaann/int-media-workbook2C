PERSONAL PROJECT

SENTIMIRROR

Sentimirror is a web-based interactive artwork that visualises a user's emotional state through colour, motion an silhouette interaction.

The experience begins with the user answering a poetic question (eg "What do you feel when you close your eyes).

Their response is analysed using ML5 sentiment analysis.

Based on this input, the system generates visuals of waves of colour that change with the user's movements.

For this project I will be working in a pair with Tashya David.

GUAI @ Science Gallery

Mindy Meng Wang & Monica Lim

This interactive AI game was quite a surreal experience.

This work uses emotion recognition biometric AI to analyse your personal attributes and match you to a fantastical monster avatar from the Chinese mythical text Classic of Mountains and Seas.

There were various aspects of this work that deserve appreciation and made the work come alive into an immersive experience including the dim environment, bright neon lights, sound.

This is probably my current favourite interactive artwork. And I hope to draw some inspiration from this and incorporate it into my final project.

Text Rain

Romy Achituv & Camille Utterback

Text rain is an interactive installation where viewers move their body to lift and play with falling letters.

While it's a simple visual, I find it interesting because of it's ability to let viewers collect letter which can sometimes form a word. Also, these words are actually a poem, making the artwork more meaningful and giving the viewers a reason to stay for longer and engage with it.
